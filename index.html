Champagne and Sparkling

"Aube champagne, of course! (But not only.)"

To the question: "What is a good champagne?", the answer is, of course, "A champagne from Aube", a chauvinistic quip tinged with a hint of provocation. Although Aube is the second-largest Champagne-producing department in France, there is no real hierarchy between production areas. After all, geographical origin isn't everything. Between the different grape varieties, terroirs, dosages, vintages, brands and other cuvées, choosing your champagne can quickly turn into a headache.

Whisky and Spirit

The value of a whisky is judged by its flavor, which should be neither too bitter nor too sweet. It should be at an optimum level to bring out all its best facets: a malty, slightly spicy, fruity or woody taste and a fragrance that describes its origins. If you're curious or an enthusiast, don't hesitate to take a look at our spirits catalog. We have a number of references in stock, including sure-fire favourites as well as lesser-known spirits that we'd like to highlight.
